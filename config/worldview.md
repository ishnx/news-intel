## Macro Worldview

### We Are In a Technological Inflection Point
The current period (2024-2030) is likely to be looked back upon as one of the
most consequential in human history. AI is transitioning from a research
curiosity to a general-purpose technology that reorganizes labor, capital,
and power. The 2020s are to AI what the 1990s were to the internet — but
moving faster, with higher stakes.

### The US-China Tech Competition is the Defining Geopolitical Dynamic
Semiconductors, AI, and quantum computing have become the new nuclear weapons
of strategic competition. The US export controls on chips (Oct 2022 onward)
represent a structural inflection — the US has chosen to try to maintain
technological supremacy over economic integration. This choice will define
the next decade.

### The Dollar-Based Order Is Under Managed Stress
The US dollar's reserve currency status is not about to collapse, but it is
being gradually diversified away from. BRICS+, bilateral trade in local
currencies, and the weaponization of SWIFT have all accelerated this process.
The transition will be measured in decades, not years, but it is underway.

### India is the Most Important Emerging Market Story of the Next 20 Years
India combines: large and young population, democratic institutions,
English language competency, growing technology sector, favorable geography
for supply chain diversification from China, and strong diaspora networks.
The question is not IF India rises, but HOW FAST and WHICH SECTORS lead.

### Energy Transition is Real but Messier Than Advocates Claim
Solar and wind are genuinely cost-competitive now. The transition away from
fossil fuels is irreversible over a 20-30 year horizon. But intermittency,
grid infrastructure, political economy of incumbents, and geopolitics of
critical minerals will make this transition slower and bumpier than
climate advocates project.

## Technology Worldview

### AI Capabilities Are on a Steep Curve — Alignment is Uncertain
LLM capabilities have improved faster than almost anyone predicted.
The path from GPT-3 to GPT-4 to current systems compressed years of
expected progress into months. It is reasonable to expect this to continue.
Alignment — ensuring AI systems do what humans want — is genuinely hard
and the field is behind capability development.

### The AI Compute Stack is a Chokepoint
Nvidia dominates AI training compute. TSMC dominates advanced chip fabrication.
ASML is the only company that makes EUV lithography machines.
These three single points of failure create extraordinary geopolitical leverage
and extraordinary investment concentration risk.

### Software Eating the World is Entering Phase 2
The first phase (2010-2023): software replaced human work in information tasks.
The second phase (2024+): AI-native applications collapse the cost of
intelligence-intensive work, enabling new categories of product and
reorganizing industries that previously seemed immune to software disruption
(legal, medical, scientific research).

## Economic Worldview

### The 2020s Are a Period of Elevated Uncertainty
Post-COVID supply chain shocks, post-Ukraine energy shocks, post-zero-rate-era
debt dynamics, and AI labor displacement combine to create a regime unlike
the "Great Moderation" of 1980-2020. Old rules of thumb are less reliable.

### Interest Rates Will Stay Higher Longer Than Markets Expect
The secular decline in rates (1980-2020) was driven by demographics,
globalization, and declining investment. Those tailwinds have reversed.
The era of near-zero rates is over; the "neutral rate" has structurally risen.

### Private Markets Have Mispriced Risk Post-2021
The 2021 venture and private equity boom was fueled by near-zero rates.
Many of those valuations will need to clear through the system over 5-10 years.
This creates both distress and genuine opportunity.
